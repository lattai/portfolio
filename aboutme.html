Circuits

Today I learned more about circuits and the parts involved. I am very excited for this unit of robotics, especially because I am not as familiar with circuits and breadboards. My group of 5 was able to identify and define parts of the circuit, and proceed in creating circuits using the Board of Education board. We created a blinking LED, and solid LED, added buttons, and created 2 LEDs in series and in parallel. Unfortunately, we were unable to add in the piezo, but we believe it might be because we had not programed the arduino. None the less, it was really cool to see how the circuits worked.


We talked a lot about robots and their ethics today. How did your idea of robotics change? What makes you excited about the future of robotics? Nervous?

Robots are cool and exciting, but they are meant to replicate and replace human activities. I look forward to robotic advancements that with improved accuracy and precision help people where human error can fail, but not quite to a place where people are put out of work because of them.


You worked on circuits in groups, and will be working in groups for the rest of the week. What worked well? What could you do better next time?

Words can't quite explain how excited I am for this unit due to my love for robotics. Because of this, I was an eager and speedy learner, and at times I could be unintentionally taking over projects. I try fixing this by asking my group mates if they have questions, or what they've found, or if they want to try, but sometimes, their answers aren't as enthusiastic as mine. It is very much something I need to improve upon.
